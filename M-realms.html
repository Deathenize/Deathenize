Realms

So, what exactly are realms?


They're like portal worlds where all inhabitants reside. Each realm has its own aesthetics, geography, and certain kinds of culture and traits attached to them, as well as rules and a ceirten type of government ruling system. Here we discuss the basics of the 7 main realms. Later on, Fyou can delve more into the specific realms by clicking on the topic buttons in the menu


* note: When we discuss individual realms, we cover at least 5 main subjects:*


Species - The kinds of inhabitants that live there.

Geography - The relationship inhabitants have with their environment, with more discussion about the world’s surface etc

Culture - Typical traits, aesthetics, and special holidays that belong to the realm and its inhabitants.

Tech - How advanced that particular society is. Do they live in the past or the future, or maybe both?

Leadership What leaders rule the realm, who takes control, and what kind of government system is in place there.


You will get to know more about these by reading more about the individual realms. It is important to keep these topics in mind when discussing the world-building or structure of the realms, as they contain pretty much important information.

Short Descriptions of the 7 Realms Starting, of course, with the:

Ethereal Realm


- Also called: The Eths, Angels, The Skydwellers
• Danger level:

Description: An angelic realm that has beautiful, classy, snobbish angels living alongside very precise types, in a heaven-like, golden place.



Earthkin Realm


- Also called: Humans, Earthdwellers, The Kins/EK
• Danger level:

Description: A human-like realm with nature and physics similar to the human world, as well as earth-like creatures that roam the land.

Mythical Realm


- Also called: Myths, A Uniel (old name reffered to by eartkins)
• Danger level:

Description: Species based on mythology and fairy tales, mostly inhabited by humanoid, animal-like, and fantasy creatures such as elves and gnomes.

Lunarian Realm/Dream realm


-Also called: Dreamite Creatures, Paralysis Inhabitants
• Danger level:

Description: Hard to make sense what happens in there. inhabited by beings that are the embodiments of dreams and nightmares or weirdcoreish like creatures

Eldritch Realm


- Also called: The Elts, Els
• Danger level:

Description: Weird, morbid creatures akin to Lovecraftian horror. They could look quite humanoid or morbid.



About Universal Arbiters

They're like realm creators or time balancers. These are the upper heads of each realm. They are also referred to as the judges of law and order, or the guardians created by Themis. They keep realms in check and make sure that "forbidden rules" are not executed. There are 2 Universal Arbiters:

Universal Balancers


- Also called: The Keepers, The Order law fate an Judgement
• Danger level: X

Description: - Currently, Universal Balancers are in power of the Wheel of Fate. They are in control of all timelines, past and future law, and fate. They ensure that everything is in order. - An important additional knowledge is that they also took part in the creation of the main 5 realms. But there's a strict rule they must follow (by the rules of Themis): they are not allowed to interfere with realm events or activities that can make or break the future of realms. All of it should be decided by hard work and natural selection, unless something strange takes place—things that are not supposed to happen or be there, events that need fixing after being tampered with, for example. Time decides what takes place, and time decides who survives and who ends up being crushed.

The Wicked


- Also called: The Cursed and the Banished
• Danger level: X

Description: - You could say they're the polar opposites of the Universal Balancers. They disagree on how things operate within the decision-making of the realms. They are mostly against the law of Themis. They were either abandoned, betrayed, or turned corrupted. Their motives can differ or be similar to each other (depends on the Wicked). Each of them took part in the creation of the main 5 realms (a 50-50 kind of bargain, you could say). Though their deeds are not always done with good intentions, they might feel sadistic, mischievous, or angry. They will often try to disrupt time or fate, or mess and toy with the realms and their own inhabitants, causing disruption. Each of these two will gain their own section as well, where we will delve in detail about their respective topics.
